The Alexandria Clinical Oncology AI & Translational Research Lab (ACO-AI Lab) is a pioneering initiative dedicated to transforming cancer care through artificial intelligence. Established within the Department of Clinical Oncology at the Faculty of Medicine, Alexandria University, the lab serves as a nexus between advanced computational science and clinical oncology—with a strong emphasis on real-world translation and impact.

The ACO-AI Lab is uniquely positioned to address one of the most critical gaps in global oncology innovation: the adaptation of cutting-edge AI technologies to healthcare systems with constrained resources. By developing and validating AI-driven solutions across drug discovery, diagnosis, medical imaging, and personalized treatment planning, the lab ensures that innovation is not only scientifically robust but also clinically applicable within the Egyptian healthcare system.

A central pillar of the lab's mission is the localization of artificial intelligence—designing systems that reflect regional epidemiology, clinical workflows, and population-specific characteristics, transforming global oncology knowledge into practical, scalable tools effectively deployed in Egypt and similar healthcare environments.

Beyond national impact, the ACO-AI Lab is envisioned as a regional hub for AI-driven oncology across Africa and the Middle East, leading collaborative research, capacity building, and technology transfer efforts to ensure that advancements in cancer care reach underserved populations.

Flagship Initiative

AI Drug Discovery Platform

Our primary active grant focuses on building a next-generation AI-driven drug discovery platform, purpose-built for the Egyptian oncology context and designed to serve underserved communities across the region.

Active Grant · Drug Discovery
Egyptian AI Drug Discovery & Treatment Adaptation Platform

A comprehensive, end-to-end AI platform for oncology drug discovery and treatment guideline adaptation—engineered to reflect the unique genomic landscape, clinical workflows, and resource constraints of Egyptian and MENA populations.

Egyptian-Context AI Models
Training and validating ML/DL models on Egyptian patient cohorts—accounting for local tumor biology, genetic variants, and population-specific disease profiles that differ from Western datasets.
Drug Repurposing Engine
Identifying cost-effective repurposing candidates using multi-omics integration (genomics, proteomics, transcriptomics) to deliver accessible treatments for Egyptian patients.
Guideline Localization
Adapting international treatment protocols (NCCN, ESMO, ASCO) to Egyptian clinical realities—accounting for available drugs, diagnostics, infrastructure, and patient demographics.
Rural & Underserved Deployment
Lightweight, offline-capable AI decision-support tools deployable in low-bandwidth, resource-limited settings across rural Egypt and the broader MENA region.
Platform Architecture
Multi-Omics Integration Engine

Fusing genomic, proteomic, transcriptomic, and clinical data streams to identify novel therapeutic targets and predict drug response in the Egyptian patient population—bridging precision medicine with regional accessibility.

Equity Focus
Underserved Community Outreach

Deploying AI tools specifically optimized for Egypt's rural governorates and underserved urban areas—including Upper Egypt, Delta region communities, and border areas with limited oncology infrastructure.

Regulatory & Validation
Egyptian Healthcare System Alignment

Ensuring all platform outputs align with Egyptian Ministry of Health & Population (MoHP) frameworks, national formularies, and Egyptian insurance coverage realities to maximize real-world applicability.
